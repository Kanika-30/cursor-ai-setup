# Dave Gerhardt

## Brief Introduction

Dave Gerhardt is a B2B SaaS marketing leader, creator, author, and community builder best known for making practical SaaS marketing ideas easy to understand and apply. He became widely known through his work at Drift, where he helped build one of the strongest B2B SaaS brands of the 2010s, and later through Exit Five, his media and community company for B2B marketers.

His current role is Founder of Exit Five, a newsletter, podcast, private community, job board, event platform, and content hub for B2B marketers. Exit Five describes itself as a newsletter and community built specifically for B2B marketers, with 40,000+ newsletter readers and 5,700+ members in its private community.

## Biggest Achievements in B2B SaaS

Dave's biggest achievements are tied to building memorable B2B SaaS brands, media programs, and communities:

- **Drift:** Dave was one of the early marketing leaders at Drift and helped popularize its conversational marketing category, its strong brand voice, and its use of content, podcasts, and founder-led storytelling.
- **Privy:** He later served as CMO at Privy, an ecommerce marketing platform, where he continued applying brand, content, and demand generation principles in a SaaS environment.
- **Exit Five:** He built Exit Five into one of the most recognized communities for B2B marketers, covering topics such as demand generation, brand, content, product marketing, AI in marketing, marketing leadership, ABM, and growth.
- **DGMG / Dave Gerhardt Marketing Group:** Before Exit Five became the main brand, Dave built a paid community and content business around practical B2B marketing advice.
- **Books and education:** Dave has published marketing books and courses, including work around founder-led brand building and B2B marketing leadership.

His most important contribution to B2B SaaS marketing is showing that marketing does not need to sound corporate to be effective. He helped normalize plain-language B2B content, founder-led storytelling, strong points of view, and organic social distribution as serious growth levers.

## Why Is He So Influential?

Thousands of B2B marketers follow Dave because his content feels like advice from someone who has actually operated inside fast-growing SaaS companies. He avoids overly theoretical marketing language and usually explains ideas in simple, memorable terms.

His content is different from many B2B creators because it is:

- **Plainspoken:** He writes like a practitioner, not a textbook.
- **Operator-led:** His advice comes from building brands, teams, communities, and campaigns, not just commenting on them.
- **Useful for everyday marketers:** He focuses on real work: positioning, messaging, LinkedIn posts, newsletters, podcasts, launches, events, executive buy-in, and marketing team leadership.
- **Strongly POV-driven:** Dave's content often pushes marketers to think beyond tactics and build a recognizable point of view.
- **Community-centered:** Exit Five turns content into discussion, peer learning, job opportunities, and practical feedback.

For LinkedIn organic content strategy, Dave is influential because he demonstrates how one person can use a consistent voice, repeated ideas, useful examples, and direct audience feedback to build trust at scale.

## Major Learnings for B2B Marketers

Dave's work repeatedly teaches a few important ideas:

- **Build a brand people recognize before they are ready to buy.** Organic content, newsletters, podcasts, and community can create familiarity long before a sales conversation.
- **Make the founder or executive voice part of the marketing strategy.** Founder-led content often performs because buyers want to hear from real people, not only brand accounts.
- **Write like a human.** Strong B2B content usually sounds clear, specific, and conversational.
- **Turn one strong idea into many assets.** A single point of view can become LinkedIn posts, newsletter issues, podcast topics, event sessions, and sales enablement material.
- **Use content to teach, not just promote.** The best B2B content helps the audience do their job better before asking for anything.
- **Create category and company memory through repetition.** Dave often reinforces the same core beliefs until they become associated with his brand.
- **Community is a distribution advantage.** A trusted community gives marketers feedback, content ideas, peer proof, and direct audience insight.

Practical frameworks he regularly teaches or demonstrates include:

- Founder-led content as a marketing channel
- Building a repeatable content operating system
- Using LinkedIn posts to test ideas before turning them into larger assets
- Creating a clear company POV before creating more content
- Treating podcasts and newsletters as owned media, not side projects
- Writing content around customer pain, internal team conversations, and market observations

## Who Should Follow Him?

- **Demand Generation:** Learn how brand, community, podcasts, events, and organic LinkedIn can create demand before buyers enter a funnel.
- **Marketing Operations:** Learn why attribution alone cannot explain all marketing influence and why qualitative buyer insight matters.
- **Customer Success:** Learn how customer stories, customer language, and community conversations can become useful marketing inputs.
- **Product Marketing:** Learn how to turn positioning, messaging, launches, objections, and customer pain points into content.
- **Content Marketers:** Learn how to write more direct, useful, and repeatable B2B content across LinkedIn, newsletters, podcasts, and video.
- **Founders:** Learn how to use personal voice and founder-led content to build trust, recruit talent, support sales, and create market authority.
- **Marketing Leaders:** Learn how to build a media and community strategy that supports brand, pipeline, recruiting, partnerships, and executive credibility.

## My Key Takeaways

1. **Write like a real person.** B2B buyers are still people. Clear, conversational writing often beats polished corporate language.

2. **Build a recognizable POV.** Organic LinkedIn works better when your content repeats a clear belief about the market, not just random tips.

3. **Use LinkedIn as an idea-testing channel.** Posts can reveal which ideas resonate before investing in newsletters, podcasts, webinars, or campaigns.

4. **Turn content into a system.** A strong post can become a newsletter, podcast topic, short video, sales talking point, event theme, or community discussion.

5. **Create trust before demand is visible.** The best organic strategy educates, entertains, and helps buyers long before they are ready to book a demo.
