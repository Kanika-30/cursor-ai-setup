# April Dunford

## Brief Introduction

April Dunford is a B2B positioning expert, author, speaker, and consultant. She is best known for helping B2B technology companies clarify why their product is different, who it is for, and why customers should choose it over alternatives.

Her work matters for LinkedIn organic content strategy because strong posts are easier to write when the positioning is clear. Without positioning, content often becomes generic advice, feature promotion, or disconnected opinions.

## Current Company and Role

April Dunford runs her own positioning consulting, speaking, and education business. Her website says she helps B2B tech companies tighten their positioning and build sales pitches that drive revenue.

Based on a manual review of April Dunford's LinkedIn profile, she is currently the **Founder of Ambient Strategy**.

April is one of the most respected voices in B2B positioning and go-to-market strategy. Through Ambient Strategy, she advises B2B technology and SaaS companies on product positioning, messaging, category creation, and AI-driven marketing strategies. She is also the bestselling author of *Obviously Awesome* and *Sales Pitch*.

- **Consulting:** Her website says her process has been used with more than 200 companies, from growing startups to large companies.

## Why They Are Influential

April is influential because she makes positioning concrete. Instead of treating positioning as a vague branding exercise, she teaches it as a structured process that affects marketing, sales, messaging, category choice, competitive differentiation, and customer understanding.

For LinkedIn strategy, her influence is important because positioning gives content a sharper point of view. A company that understands its alternatives, differentiated value, ideal customers, and market category can create posts that are more specific, memorable, and useful.

## Key Learnings

- **Positioning comes before content.** LinkedIn posts are stronger when the company knows what market it is competing in and why it wins.
- **Differentiate against real alternatives.** Buyers compare you with other options, including the status quo.
- **Messaging should make value obvious.** Good content helps customers quickly understand why the product matters.
- **Sales and marketing need the same story.** Positioning should inform ads, website copy, sales decks, executive posts, and product launches.
- **Specificity beats generic claims.** Clear positioning creates sharper content angles.

## My Key Takeaways

1. **Great LinkedIn content starts upstream.** If positioning is unclear, posts will usually feel generic.

2. **Name the alternative.** Buyers need to understand what you replace or outperform.

3. **Make the value easy to repeat.** Organic content works when the audience can remember and retell the idea.

4. **Use positioning as a content filter.** Every post should reinforce who you serve, what you believe, and why your approach is different.

5. **Connect content to sales conversations.** The best LinkedIn ideas should make actual buyer conversations easier.
