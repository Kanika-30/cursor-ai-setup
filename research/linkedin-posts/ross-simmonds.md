# Ross Simmonds

## Brief Introduction

Ross Simmonds is a marketing strategist, entrepreneur, author, speaker, and investor best known for content distribution, SEO, repurposing, and B2B SaaS content strategy. He is the founder of Foundation Marketing, a marketing firm focused on B2B tech and SaaS companies.

His work is directly relevant to LinkedIn organic content because he repeatedly teaches that creating content is only part of the job. Distribution determines whether the right audience actually sees and remembers the idea.

## Current Company and Role

Ross Simmonds is the Founder and CEO of Foundation Marketing, a B2B content marketing agency specializing in content distribution, SEO, AI search optimization, and demand generation. He helps B2B SaaS companies maximize the reach and impact of their content through strategic distribution and data-driven marketing.

## Biggest Achievements

Ross's biggest achievements are tied to making content distribution a core marketing discipline:

- **Foundation Marketing:** Foundation works with B2B tech and SaaS companies on AI visibility, content strategy, SEO, social media, content repurposing, Reddit and community, and distribution.
- **Newsletter:** Ross's website lists 75,000+ newsletter subscribers.
- **Podcast:** His website lists The Ross Simmonds Show and 110,000+ podcast downloads.
- **SaaS Breakdowns:** Ross has built a collection of SaaS content marketing breakdowns.
- **Book and frameworks:** Ross is known for the idea of creating once and distributing forever, and his work around content distribution has become widely referenced by B2B marketers.
- **Speaking:** His keynote topics include content distribution, generative engine optimization, Reddit marketing, AI, and enterprise content marketing.

## Why They Are Influential

Ross is influential because he pushes marketers to stop treating publishing as the finish line. He teaches that content needs a distribution plan across search, social, newsletters, communities, partnerships, and increasingly AI-driven discovery systems.

For LinkedIn strategy, his influence is especially important because LinkedIn is one of the distribution channels where a strong idea can be tested, repackaged, and repeatedly shared in different formats.

## Key Learnings

- **Distribution is part of content strategy.** A strong post should have a plan for where and how it will spread.
- **Create once, distribute repeatedly.** One idea can become LinkedIn posts, newsletter sections, short videos, blog sections, podcast talking points, and community discussions.
- **Content should meet the audience where they already spend time.** LinkedIn, Reddit, search, newsletters, and communities all play different roles.
- **Organic growth needs both creativity and systems.** Storytelling matters, but so do processes for repurposing and distribution.
- **AI discovery is changing content strategy.** B2B marketers need to think about how brands appear in search engines, social feeds, communities, and LLM-driven answers.

## Who Should Follow Them

- **Content Marketers:** Learn how to distribute and repurpose content more effectively.
- **Demand Generation:** Learn how organic content can create repeated exposure across channels.
- **SEO Teams:** Learn how search, social, community, and AI visibility connect.
- **Founders:** Learn why publishing is only useful when the right audience sees the message.
- **Marketing Leaders:** Learn how to build content engines that compound across formats and platforms.

## My Key Takeaways

1. **Do not stop at publish.** LinkedIn should be part of a larger distribution system.

2. **Turn every strong idea into multiple assets.** One insight can work across posts, newsletters, videos, podcasts, and sales content.

3. **Treat LinkedIn as both testing and distribution.** Posts can reveal what deserves deeper investment.

4. **Build for repeat visibility.** The audience often needs to see the same idea in multiple places before it sticks.

5. **Plan for AI-era discovery.** Organic strategy now includes search, social, communities, and how AI systems learn about brands.
