# Project Title

LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

## Objective

I selected this topic because LinkedIn has become one of the most important organic channels for B2B SaaS companies. Buyers often learn from founders, marketers, sales leaders, creators, and operators long before they speak with a sales team. By studying experts who consistently share useful content, strong opinions, and practical frameworks, I wanted to understand how B2B SaaS brands can build trust, educate their audience, and create demand without relying only on paid advertising.

## Repository Structure

- `research/sources.md`: Contains the selected topic, research objective, expert shortlist, and source links used for the assignment.
- `research/linkedin-posts/`: Includes individual research notes on LinkedIn content from each expert, including their themes, observations, and practical lessons.
- `research/youtube-transcripts/`: Contains YouTube transcript-based research and summaries for selected experts.
- `research/other/`: Reserved for additional supporting research, notes, references, or materials that do not fit into the LinkedIn or YouTube categories.

## Key Marketing Learnings

1. **Strong positioning makes content more effective.** The best LinkedIn content is not random posting. It is built on a clear understanding of the audience, the problem being solved, the alternatives in the market, and the unique point of view behind the product.

2. **Educational content builds long-term trust.** The experts studied consistently teach their audience through frameworks, examples, lessons, and direct opinions. This approach helps them become trusted voices rather than only promoting a company or product.

3. **Audience research should guide content strategy.** Effective B2B SaaS content starts with knowing where buyers spend time, what they care about, what language they use, and what questions they are already asking.

4. **Distribution matters as much as creation.** High-performing organic content is often repurposed across LinkedIn, newsletters, podcasts, communities, YouTube, and company resources. The same strong idea can create value across multiple channels.

5. **Personal brands can support company growth.** Founders, executives, and subject-matter experts can use LinkedIn to explain their thinking, share lessons from experience, and create demand in a way that feels more human and credible than traditional brand-only marketing.
